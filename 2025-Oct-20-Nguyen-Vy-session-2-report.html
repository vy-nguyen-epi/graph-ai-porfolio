Interpretation

There is a strong positive relationship between Human Development Index (HDI) and life expectancy in 2020. A Pearson correlation and linear fit on the available country data show a strong, highly significant positive association: countries with higher HDI generally have higher life expectancy. A simple linear model explains a large share of the cross-country variation (R² ≈ 0.74). In practical terms, a 0.10 increase in HDI corresponds to roughly a 4-year increase in life expectancy on average.

Some countries deviate substantially from the trend — for example, Syria and Maldives have higher life expectancy than predicted by HDI, while Lesotho and Eswatini have much lower life expectancy than predicted. These outliers may reflect local health crises, epidemiological factors (e.g., high HIV/TB burden), conflict, or data/reporting differences. Correlation does not imply causation; further multivariable analysis would be required to explore mechanisms and control for confounders.
