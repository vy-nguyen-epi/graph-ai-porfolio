Introduction

This project analyzes the job market for data analysts by looking at years of experience, programming language, and median salary from 400 job posts. The data are originally scraped from Glassdoor in June 2020. The scraping project and full dataset can be found here.



Years of Experience Analysis


Interpretation

Among 278 job posts listing minimum years of experience from 0 to 16, salaries are widely dispersed for all minimum-year values — variability within each year is large compared with differences in medians across years. The median-by-year black line is nearly flat. The small Pearson r ≈ -0.015 and Spearman ρ ≈ -0.027 indicate no meaningful linear or monotonic relationship between posted minimum years of experience and median salary. Experience alone does not explain most salary variation in this dataset and is not a strong predictor of salary here; role, skills, seniority level beyond the posted minimum, location, and company factors likely drive pay more.
