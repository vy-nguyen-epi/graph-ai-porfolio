Introduction

This project analyzes the job market for data analysts by looking at years of experience, programming language, and median salary from 400 job posts. The data are originally scraped from Glassdoor in June 2020. The scraping project and full dataset can be found here.



Years of Experience Analysis


Interpretation (focused on 015 years / 0160,000 USD):

This view restricts the x-axis to 015 years and the y-axis to 0160,000 USD to focus on the early-career range. In this subset there are 278 observations and the Pearson correlation between minimum years of experience and median salary is approximately -0.02, i.e. effectively zero. In other words, within the first 15 years the data show almost no linear relationship between the posted minimum years of experience and the median salary salaries vary substantially at all levels in this band, suggesting other factors (role, location, skills) have larger influence on pay.

Overall, the median salaries across all categories are similar to one another, suggesting that perhaps other tools (e.g., SQL, Excel, PowerBI), other job requirements, seniority, role, location, or company size likely confound the relationship.
